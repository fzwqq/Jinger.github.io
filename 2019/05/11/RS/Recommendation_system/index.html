Comparison of three recommendation systems based on collaborative filtering neural network model

Posted on 2019-05-11 | In Recommendation System

Proposal

1. Problem

In this era of information overload, information consumers and information producers have encountered enormous challenges. For the movie industry, as an information consumer, it is difficult to find out what you are interested in from a large number of movies on the video site. Because sometimes users are not sure what they want to see, which means they can’t use a search engine to find a particular movie.

The recommendation system is an intuitive defense against excessive consumer choice. In view of explosive growth of the information provided online, users are often subject to countless products or movies. Therefore, personalization is an essential strategy to promote better user experience. All in all, these systems play a vital and indispensable role in various information access systems to improve business and facilitate the decision-making process.

2. Data

The MovieLens datasets are full of data describing how people rate movies.

The MovieLens datasets, first released in 1998, describe people’s expressed preferences for movies. These preferences take the form of <user, item, rating, timestamp> tuples, each the result of a person expressing a preference (a 0–5 star rating) for a movie at a particular time. These preferences were entered by way of the MovieLens website—a recommender system that asks its users to give movie ratings in order to receive personalized movie recommendations.

3. Method

This research will be implemented on Mac OS, using development tools such as Pycharm, Anaconda and Jupyter notebook. The development language will choose Python, using the deep learning framework Pytorch. Third-party modules include but are not limited to Numpy, Scipy, Pandas, Scikit-Learn. The research will use three algorithms based on collaborative filtering, including the film recommendation system based on SVD collaborative filtering algorithm, text convolutional neural networks based on collaborative filtering techniques, and neural network with embeddings Network collaborative filtering to develop the recommendation system.

4. Analysis

Through the system, three functions will be implemented, including recommending the same type of movie, the user’s favorite movie, and the movie that the person who recommends watching the movie also likes to watch. Three collaborative filtering-based recommendation systems will be implemented on the Movielens dataset. It consists mainly of four parts. The first step is to get the Movielens dataset, and the second step is to explore the data and build and view the user’s project matrix. The third step is to implement three collaborative filtering recommendation systems based on the above three algorithms, the fourth step of model training and the analysis of prediction results. Finally, the study will compare and analyze the prediction effects of the algorithms and analyze their respective strengths and weaknesses.
